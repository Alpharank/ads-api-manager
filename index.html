Google Ads Metrics

Impressions The number of times your ad was displayed on a search result page or website. Each time your ad appears, it counts as one impression.
Clicks The number of times users clicked on your ad. Includes clicks to your website, app, or call extension.
CTR (Click-Through Rate) Clicks ÷ Impressions × 100 The percentage of impressions that resulted in a click. Higher CTR indicates more relevant ad copy and targeting.
Cost (Spend) The total amount spent on ad clicks for the selected period. This is the actual amount charged by Google Ads.
Avg. CPC (Cost Per Click) Cost ÷ Clicks The average amount you pay each time someone clicks your ad. Varies by keyword competition and quality score.
Google Conv. Actions defined in Google Ads as valuable (e.g., form submissions, phone calls, account sign-ups). Uses data-driven attribution, so fractional values are possible when credit is shared across multiple clicks.
Google Conv. Rate Google Conv. ÷ Clicks × 100 The percentage of clicks that resulted in a Google Ads conversion. Measures how effectively clicks turn into desired actions.
CPA (Cost per Google Conv.) Cost ÷ Google Conv. The average cost to acquire one Google Ads conversion. Lower CPA means more efficient ad spend.

First-Party Attribution Metrics

Apps (Applications) Number of loan or membership applications started. Tracked via GCLID click ID linking Google Ads clicks to your application system.
Approved Number of applications that were approved. Attributed to the ad click that initiated the customer journey.
Funded Number of applications that reached funded status. This is your primary conversion event from first-party data.
CPF (Cost Per Funded) Cost ÷ Funded The cost to acquire one funded application. More accurate than CPA as it uses first-party conversion data.
Value (Revenue) Total dollar value of funded applications. Calculated from loan amounts and expected lifetime value.
Total Funded Value Sum of Value Total dollar value of all funded applications. Shows the aggregate revenue attributed to each campaign or keyword.
ROAS (Return on Ad Spend) (Value ÷ Cost) − 1 Return on ad spend using first-party revenue data. Red if below 1.5×, green if above 4.0×.

Attribution Models

Last Click 100% credit to the last ad clicked before conversion. Best for understanding which ads close the deal. Default model.
First Click 100% credit to the first ad clicked in the customer journey. Best for understanding which ads introduce new customers.
Linear Equal credit split across all touchpoints. If 3 ads were clicked, each gets 33% credit. Best for understanding the full journey.
